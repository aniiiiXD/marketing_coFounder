Eterna Target Audience Analysis

PRIMARY TARGET SEGMENTS:

1. RETAIL TRADERS
Demographics:
- Age: 25-45 years old
- Income: $50K-$200K annually
- Education: College-educated, often in finance, tech, or business
- Experience: Intermediate to advanced traders
- Location: Global, with focus on US, EU, and APAC markets

Characteristics:
- Seeking professional-grade trading tools
- Interested in options and derivatives trading
- Value advanced analytics and risk management
- Want institutional-quality execution speeds
- Desire educational resources and community features
- Price-conscious but willing to pay for quality
- Tech-savvy and comfortable with digital platforms

Pain Points:
- Limited access to advanced options trading tools
- High fees on existing platforms
- Poor execution speeds and slippage
- Lack of sophisticated risk management tools
- Limited educational resources for complex strategies
- Fragmented trading experience across multiple platforms

2. HIGH-FREQUENCY TRADING FIRMS
Demographics:
- Company size: 5-500 employees
- Trading volume: $1M-$1B+ daily
- Geographic focus: Global financial centers
- Technology focus: Quantitative and algorithmic trading

Characteristics:
- Require ultra-low latency execution
- Need comprehensive API and SDK access
- Demand institutional-grade infrastructure
- Value customizable trading solutions
- Require robust risk management systems
- Need reliable market data feeds
- Seek cost-effective trading infrastructure

Pain Points:
- High infrastructure costs
- Complex integration requirements
- Limited perpetual options market access
- Expensive market data and connectivity
- Regulatory compliance complexity
- Need for multiple vendor relationships

SECONDARY TARGET SEGMENTS:

3. PROFESSIONAL INDIVIDUAL TRADERS
- Former institutional traders
- High-net-worth individuals
- Sophisticated retail traders transitioning to professional status
- Seeking institutional tools with retail accessibility

4. SMALL TO MEDIUM TRADING FIRMS
- Proprietary trading firms
- Hedge funds under $100M AUM
- Family offices with active trading strategies
- Regional trading companies

5. FINTECH DEVELOPERS AND INTEGRATORS
- Companies building trading applications
- Financial advisors seeking trading infrastructure
- Robo-advisors requiring options capabilities
- Educational platforms teaching advanced trading

MARKET SIZING:
- Retail options traders: ~2M active globally
- HFT firms: ~1,000 globally
- Professional individual traders: ~50K globally
- Small trading firms: ~5,000 globally

GEOGRAPHIC PRIORITIES:
Primary: United States, United Kingdom, Singapore
Secondary: European Union, Canada, Australia
Tertiary: Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea

MESSAGING PRIORITIES BY SEGMENT:
Retail: "Professional trading tools made accessible"
HFT: "Enterprise infrastructure with startup innovation"
Professional: "Bridge the gap between retail and institutional"
SME Firms: "Scale your trading operations efficiently"
